## Exterior Architecture

### Description
The Nutrition Farm-Structure is the only "alive" building in Balencia City -- partially covered in cascading vertical farms that give it a green, growing presence unique among the dark tech-forward district buildings. The form is tiered: each floor steps back from the one below, creating a stepped pyramid profile where each tier holds a horizontal farm layer under warm amber grow lights. Cascading green plants spill over tier edges, and the amber glow lights create a warm halo effect visible from distance.

Glass greenhouse sections punctuate the tiered facade -- transparent enclosures showing hydroponic growing systems within. An open market area at ground level invites entry: a covered but open-sided space with amber-lit produce displays visible through the glass walls. The building blends organic curves into its structural form, softening the tier edges with rounded transitions rather than hard steps. A kitchen chimney/ventilation element on the roof provides vertical accent. Water irrigation channels run visibly along the exterior tier edges -- thin channels carrying water down the stepped form. The overall impression is warmth, nourishment, and organic vitality in a city of dark glass and metal.

### Key Architectural Elements
- Tiered main structure: 12 floors stepping back, creating stepped pyramid profile

- Cascading green plants spilling over tier edges (simplified foliage geometry)

### Silhouette Markers

## Interior -- Nourishment Hall

### Description
The Nourishment Hall is a warm, inviting communal space centered around shared dining. Open communal tables (long, low, wooden-toned surfaces) run down the center of the room, flanked by ambient amber lighting that creates a golden atmosphere unlike the cooler palettes of other district interiors. Holographic nutrition breakdowns float beside dishes at each table position: small translucent displays showing macro rings (concentric circles for protein/carb/fat) and micro bars (small bar charts for vitamins/minerals).

An AI nutrition station occupies one end: a standing counter where meals are scanned and analyzed, with holographic cross-day nutritional connections displayed above. A living market fills the opposite end: floating shelves arranged as a walkable produce display with warm spotlights and holographic nutritional tags on each item. An adaptive calorie wall on one side breathes gently -- a large surface that adjusts its display based on cross-district activity data. Chef prep zones with holographic recipe guides line the back: kitchen workstations with floating instruction panels above. Hydration stations with glass carafe forms and holographic water intake rings sit at room corners.

### Focal Point
The living market -- a multi-level floating shelf produce display approximately 6m wide and 3m deep, with warm amber spotlights on each shelf and holographic nutritional tags floating beside each item, the most visually rich and warm element in the room.
