## Exterior Architecture

### Description
The Yoga Sanctuary floats above a calm reflecting lake -- the only structure in Balencia City not built on solid ground. Organic curves define every surface: no sharp corners, no aggressive angles. The main platform hovers on thick support pillars that rise from the water surface, their forms tapered and smooth like tree trunks. Multiple glass domes of varying sizes (2-3 primary domes) cap different sections of the sanctuary, their surfaces partially transparent to reveal interior gardens.

Living walls with bioluminescent vegetation patches cover exposed exterior surfaces, creating a soft sage glow that pulses on a slow breathing cycle. Hanging gardens and trailing vines drape from the edges of open-air terraces. Gentle floating walkways connect the dome sections -- thin curved paths with low railings that arc gently over the water. The reflecting lake below mirrors the structure, doubling its visual presence. The entire composition communicates stillness, breath, and organic growth.

## Interior -- Main Yoga Dome

### Description
The largest dome interior is a single open space with a translucent ceiling filtering soft sage-tinted light. The floor is a warm natural surface -- smooth but not cold. At the center, 12 yoga mat positions are arranged in a circle, each marked by a faint sage glow ring on the floor. Breathing energy rings expand and contract on a 4-second cycle from the center point, visible as translucent concentric circles that grow outward and fade. Meditation gardens occupy the edges: still water pools with smooth stones and floating amber lanterns that drift slowly above the water surface. Mindfulness chambers line one wall -- float-like pod forms (elongated oval capsules) with translucent shells. An emotional healing gathering dome (a smaller dome-within-a-dome) at the far end projects mood landscape holograms on its inner surface.
