## Exterior Architecture

### Description
The Data Cathedral is the most visually complex building in Balencia City because its walls ARE data. The overall form follows a cathedral-style architecture -- tall, pointed, with a soaring central spire/pinnacle -- but every facade surface is covered with embedded data displays showing flowing charts, dashboards, and metric visualizations. Teal LED data streams flow across the exterior walls like digital blood through veins, constantly moving and updating. The building never looks the same twice because the surface is alive.

Flying buttress-style data conduits extend outward from the main body at several heights -- structural arcs that double as data transmission pathways, visually connecting the cathedral to the city's data network. Stained glass-style data windows punctuate the facade: large pointed-arch window forms filled not with colored glass but with holographic data panels showing abstract visualizations. The entrance is framed by data waterfalls -- vertical streams of teal light cascading down both sides of the doorway. Observation platforms extend at various heights along the buttresses, providing vantage points. The central spire/pinnacle carries a data beacon at its apex.

### Key Architectural Elements
- Cathedral-style main structure: 30 floors, tall pointed form, soaring proportions
- Facade data displays: flowing chart/dashboard textures on all wall surfaces (emissive)
- Teal LED data streams: flowing light paths across exterior walls (energy slot)
- Flying buttress data conduits: 4-6 structural arcs extending outward from body
- Central spire/pinnacle: tall pointed crown above roofline with data beacon
- Stained glass-style data windows: 6-8 large pointed-arch forms with holo panels
- Data waterfall entrance framing: vertical teal light streams flanking doorway
- Observation platforms on buttresses: small flat extensions at varying heights
- Data beacon at spire apex: teal light pulse at top
- Buttress base anchors at ground level: heavy foundation where arcs meet ground

### Silhouette Markers
Cathedral with data-covered facade -- the only building with "living" walls. The pointed spire, flying buttresses, and pointed-arch windows evoke cathedral architecture, but the constant data flow on every surface makes it unmistakable. No other building has external buttress arcs or stained-glass-style data windows.

## Interior -- Data Sanctum

### Description
The interior is a cathedral nave reinterpreted as a data visualization temple. Massive 3D charts float at different heights throughout the soaring vertical space -- bar charts, scatter plots, and network graphs rendered as physical light objects occupying the volume. A life-analytics timeline stretches the full length of the nave: a 3D landscape where orange represents effort, green represents achievement, and purple represents AI assistance, creating a topographic data terrain.

A D3.js-style neural network graph dominates one wall -- a massive network visualization with nodes that fire and bloom with teal light as connections activate. 365-day habit heatmaps tile another wall as grid panels of varying color intensity. Emotional wave patterns flow along a third wall as continuous sine curves in shifting colors. Prediction models branch upward like trees of light: forking future paths where the brightest branch represents the most probable outcome. The ceiling displays a live city system map showing all districts with real-time status data -- a top-down view of the entire Balencia City.

### Focal Point
The life-analytics timeline -- a full-length 3D landscape stretching the entire nave (approximately 15m long, 3m wide, 2m tall) with topographic data terrain in orange (effort), green (achievement), and purple (AI), the single most data-dense visualization in the building.

### Supporting Props (7 items)
- Floating 3D charts: 4-6 chart forms at varying heights (bar charts, scatter plots as geometry)
- Neural network graph wall: large flat surface with node-and-edge network visualization
- 365-day habit heatmap panels: 2 grid-tile wall sections with varying-intensity cells
- Emotional wave pattern wall: curved surface with flowing sine wave geometry
- Prediction model trees: 2 branching upward structures (forking paths of light)
- Ceiling city system map: overhead flat panel showing top-down Balencia City layout
- Data interaction pedestals: 3 standing-height cylindrical stations for querying data

### Light Empties
- light_0: Above life-analytics timeline center -- teal key light illuminating data terrain
- light_1: Behind neural network wall -- intense teal backlight for node activation glow
- light_2: At ceiling map level -- ambient fill casting gentle teal down-light

### Camera Target
Standing in the nave facing the life-analytics timeline at eye height, with floating 3D charts above, neural network wall visible to one side, and the ceiling city map overhead. The cathedral vertical space should be felt in the framing.
