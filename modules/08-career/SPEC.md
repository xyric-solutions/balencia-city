## Exterior Architecture

### Description
The Career district is a cluster of sleek interconnected towers where everything points upward -- a visual metaphor for professional ascent. The main tower reaches 40 floors (the tallest district building, though still less than half the SIA Tower), flanked by 2-3 secondary towers at 25-35 floors each. Every architectural line and surface angle directs the eye upward: tapered forms, upward-pointing edges, and vertical accent strips.

Enclosed sky-bridges connect the towers at multiple heights, creating functional bridges between career stages. Transparent elevator tubes are visible on the exterior -- vertical glass cylinders where people visibly ascend, reinforcing the upward narrative. Electric blue LED edge lighting traces every floor joint on every tower, creating a precise horizontal banding that contrasts with the vertical thrust. The main tower crown features an executive observation deck -- a cantilevered glass platform extending outward. A ground-level networking plaza connects all tower bases with open-plan gathering spaces. All towers share the same architectural language: clean, precise, professional.

### Key Architectural Elements
- Main tower: 40 floors, tallest, sleek tapered form, crown observation deck
- Secondary towers: 2-3 at 25-35 floors, same design language but shorter
- Enclosed sky-bridges between towers: 2-3 at different heights (glass-enclosed)
- Transparent elevator tubes on exterior: vertical glass cylinders on main + 1 secondary tower
- Blue LED edge lighting on every floor joint: horizontal emissive bands on all towers
- Executive observation deck: cantilevered glass platform on main tower crown
- Ground-level networking plaza: open flat area connecting all tower bases
- Upward-pointing architectural elements: tapered edges, angled fins on roof corners
- Pipeline hardpoint on main tower crown for SIA energy conduit
- Entry lobby visible at ground level: double-height glass facade on main tower

### Silhouette Markers
Tower cluster with sky-bridges. Second tallest district building (main tower 40 floors) but still notably shorter than SIA. Distinguished from Communication Hub (#05) by clean observation decks (no antennas), taller main tower, and visible elevator tubes. The upward taper of every tower and the connecting bridges create a climbing, aspirational silhouette.

## Interior -- Executive Command Hub

### Description
The main tower ground floor operates as an executive command hub -- a precision-designed workspace where professional growth is visualized in every surface. AI career advisor workstations line the perimeter: sleek standing-desk forms with holographic projection surfaces above each showing career trajectory data. Growth charts dominate the walls -- ascending blue lines with orange milestone markers that trace upward paths, each representing a career progression arc.

Deep-focus productivity zones occupy alcoves along the sides: enclosed booths with floating task boards (translucent panels showing prioritized work items). A business strategy room with a 3D interactive map table sits at the rear -- a large flat surface projecting a holographic terrain of market opportunities. Networking sky-bridge interiors are visible at the upper level, where business card exchanges happen as light-pulse transfers between people. Skill growth trees branch upward from floor-mounted bases: vertical branching structures where each branch represents a skill domain and glows brighter with mastery.

### Focal Point
The growth charts on the main wall -- a 10m wide display surface showing multiple ascending blue career trajectory lines with orange milestone markers, the dominant visual statement about upward professional momentum.
