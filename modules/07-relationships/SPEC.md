## Exterior Architecture

### Description
The Relationships Garden is the anti-tower -- deliberately low, curved, and human-scaled. While every other district reaches for the sky, this building spreads outward and stays close to the ground, communicating intimacy and approachability. Only 15 floors at its peak, it is the shortest district building in Balencia City. The form is organic and flowing, with no sharp corners or aggressive angles. Curved walls sweep gently, and garden terraces cascade down the exterior in green and rose-tinted layers.

Water features surround the entire building -- a shallow moat of still, reflective water that creates a sense of separation and serenity. Intimate walkways and small bridges cross the water at multiple points, connecting the garden to the surrounding city. Rose garden areas with low organic planters cluster near the entrances. Transparent dome sections punctuate the roof, revealing warm-lit interior gathering spaces. The building glows with a gentle, inviting warmth -- not the aggressive energy of the competitive or professional districts, but the soft pulse of connection. A single connection bridge extends outward, linking the garden ecosystem to adjacent districts.

## Interior -- Connection Gardens

### Description
The interior is a garden-first space -- more landscape than architecture. Walking paths wind through the room, lined with rose-colored energy threads whose brightness varies with bond strength (bright for strong connections, dim for new ones). AI relationship insight benches are positioned along the paths: simple seating forms with small holographic displays above showing connection data.

A family bonding dome occupies the central space -- a transparent dome-within-a-room where warm rose threads connect the people within, visible through the glass shell. Trust vines climb the walls and ceiling: organic tendril forms whose thickness represents relationship strength (thick for deep trust, thin for developing connections). Memory timeline walkways wind along the perimeter -- paths flanked by holographic abstract moments: soft shapes of light representing shared experiences. Empathy spaces are tucked into alcoves: intimate nooks where two people sit and their emotional auras (represented as overlapping colored light fields) merge and blend.

### Focal Point
